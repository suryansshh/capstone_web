What is Alzheimer's Disease?

Alzheimer's disease is a degenerative brain disorder that progressively destroys memory and cognitive skills. It is the most common cause of dementia and affects millions globally. Symptoms typically start as mild memory loss but can advance to severe impairment in reasoning, behavior, and daily functions.

Early Symptoms

  • Memory loss
  • Difficulty performing familiar tasks
  • Confusion about time or place

Advanced Symptoms

  • Severe memory impairment
  • Difficulty recognizing family or friends
  • Loss of ability to communicate

Types of Alzheimer's Disease

Early-Onset Alzheimer's

Occurs in individuals younger than 65. It is rare but progresses rapidly.

Late-Onset Alzheimer's

The most common type, typically diagnosed in individuals aged 65 and above.

Familial Alzheimer's Disease

A rare genetic form of Alzheimer's passed down in families.
